# About Eastbound Reports

## What We Do

Eastbound Reports provides accurate translations, context, and analysis of Russian media sources to English-speaking audiences. We bridge the information gap between Russian and Western media coverage, helping readers understand multiple perspectives on international events.

## Our Mission

**Transparency and Independence:**
- Everything we publish is public and transparent
- Completely independent from all governments
- We only analyze publicly available, open-source information
- Frame our work as journalism and research, not intelligence analysis

**Objectivity:**
- Translate accurately and provide cultural/political context
- Report objectively without taking partisan positions
- Acknowledge biases in all sources
- Maintain a professional, academic tone
- Never work with intelligence agencies or handle classified information

## Content Types

### Weekly Analysis Posts
Deep dives into significant Russian media stories or developments, typically 1000-1500 words covering:
- What happened and why it matters
- Russian perspectives from multiple sources
- Context that Western audiences typically miss
- Comparison with Western coverage
- Implications for policy, business, and culture

### Translation Posts
Full translations of significant Russian articles, speeches, or statements, with:
- Accurate translation preserving tone and context
- Cultural and linguistic notes
- Analysis of what the piece reveals
- Proper source citations

## Our Sources

We analyze content from:
- **Major Russian News:** TASS, RIA Novosti, Interfax, RT
- **Business Media:** Kommersant, Vedomosti, RBC
- **Analysis:** Carnegie Moscow, Telegram channels
- **Government:** Kremlin.ru, MID Russia, Duma statements

All sources are cited with links to the original Russian content.

## Who We Serve

- Policy analysts and government officials
- Journalists and researchers
- Business professionals working with Russia
- Students and educators
- Anyone interested in understanding Russian perspectives
